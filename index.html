Mount Everest
Facing Climate Crisis

6 alarming facts about how the world's highest peak is transforming due to climate change, pollution, and human impact.

10 Years of Change
01

The Mountain is Warming Twice as Fast

The Everest region has warmed by 2.0°C since 1970 — nearly double the global average of 1.1°C. In just the last decade, temperatures at Base Camp have risen from -15°C to -8°C during climbing season.

2x faster warming than global average
+7°C warmer at Base Camp today vs 2015
🌡️
Source: ICIMOD, Himalayan Climate Report 2024
02

Khumbu Glacier Has Thinned by 60 Meters

🧊

The famous Khumbu Glacier, which climbers traverse on their way to the summit, has thinned by 40-60 meters in just the past decade. Scientists predict up to 80% of Himalayan glaciers could disappear by 2100 if emissions continue unchecked.

60m glacier thickness lost in 10 years
80% projected glacier loss by 2100
Source: NASA Earth Observatory, Science Advances 2024
03

47 New Glacial Lakes Formed Since 2015

Rapid glacier melt has created 47 new glacial lakes in the Everest region that didn't exist a decade ago. These lakes are classified as "potentially dangerous" — a single glacial lake outburst flood could devastate entire valleys within hours.

47 new dangerous lakes since 2015
200+ total dangerous lakes in Nepal
🌊
Source: Nepal Department of Hydrology & Meteorology
