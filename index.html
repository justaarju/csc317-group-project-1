Mount Everest
Facing Climate Crisis

6 alarming facts about how the world's highest peak is transforming due to climate change, pollution, and human impact.

10 Years of Change
01

The Mountain is Warming Twice as Fast

The Everest region has warmed by 2.0°C since 1970 — nearly double the global average of 1.1°C. In just the last decade, temperatures at Base Camp have risen from -15°C to -8°C during climbing season.

2x faster warming than global average
+7°C warmer at Base Camp today vs 2015
🌡️
Source: ICIMOD, Himalayan Climate Report 2024
02

Khumbu Glacier Has Thinned by 60 Meters

🧊

The famous Khumbu Glacier, which climbers traverse on their way to the summit, has thinned by 40-60 meters in just the past decade. Scientists predict up to 80% of Himalayan glaciers could disappear by 2100 if emissions continue unchecked.

60m glacier thickness lost in 10 years
80% projected glacier loss by 2100
Source: NASA Earth Observatory, Science Advances 2024
03

47 New Glacial Lakes Formed Since 2015

Rapid glacier melt has created 47 new glacial lakes in the Everest region that didn't exist a decade ago. These lakes are classified as "potentially dangerous" — a single glacial lake outburst flood could devastate entire valleys within hours.

47 new dangerous lakes since 2015
200+ total dangerous lakes in Nepal
🌊
Source: Nepal Department of Hydrology & Meteorology
04

Everest is Becoming the World's Highest Landfill

🗑️

Over 50 tons of waste are removed from Everest annually, but 12-15 tons of human waste remain on the mountain each year. Microplastics have been found in snow at 8,440 meters — near the summit — from synthetic climbing gear breaking down.

50+ tons of waste removed annually
138 bodies remain on the mountain
Source: Sagarmatha Pollution Control Committee, National Geographic
05

Melting Ice Releases Decades of Toxic Pollution

Scientists have found DDT, PCBs, and other banned chemicals in snow and water samples from Everest. These "forever chemicals" were frozen for decades and are now being released as glaciers melt — contaminating water sources for 1.9 billion people downstream.

1.9B people rely on Himalayan water
50+ years of pollutants being released
☠️
Source: Science Advances, Environmental Science & Technology
06

The Summit is Actually Shrinking

📉

While officially measured at 8,848.86m in 2020, the summit's ice cap has thinned by 2-3 meters in the last decade. Permafrost melt beneath the summit and increased erosion are causing gradual settling. Some scientists estimate the true height may now be 3-4 meters lower when accounting for ice loss.

2-3m ice cap thinning since 2015
8,845m estimated current rock height
Source: Chinese Academy of Sciences, University of Maine Climate Change Institute
